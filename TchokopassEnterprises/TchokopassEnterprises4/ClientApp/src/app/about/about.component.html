Agriculture is Africa’s engine for growth.

Most African countries have determined to reduce their dependence on raw materials, particularly hydrocarbons given the drops in oil prices, and transform their economy to become an emergent nation by 2030. Agriculture accounts for nearly one-quarter of the continent’s GDP and employs almost two-thirds of its labor force, which is dominated by small holders who farm for subsistence or grow cash crops. But changes are under way. The past 15 years have witnessed deals that have led to the allocation of thousands of square kilometers of land for industrial-scale cultivation of crops, food, biofuels, and animal feed.

With international demand for cashew and oil crops such as castor, oil palm, sesame, and sunflower forecast to increase, the relative ease of access to land, lower setup costs, and government incentives are likely to fuel agricultural expansion in Africa, even in the face of challenges such as price fluctuations. In some countries, Oil palm accounts for the largest portion of active investments on the continent. In others, Cashew Trees cultivation has led to a boom in villages.

Africa produces around 40% of the estimated 2.6 million metric tons of raw cashew every year. Although Africa is the leading producers of cashews in the world, processing is at a low development stage. Major cashew nut producing countries in Africa are Nigeria, Côte d'Ivoire, Ghana, Gambia, Mali, Benin, Tanzania, Mozambique, Kenya, and Senegal. Less than 10% of the raw cashew produced in the region is processed locally and remaining is processed in foreign countries after shipment.


Tchokopas Enterprises will contribute in making African countries brake that trend by investing (B2B) in firms processing cashew in Africa or creating processing firms in Africa. That will, in term, increase the foreign exchange in African countries and bring more foreign currencies into Africa. In the process, will help creating more jobs locally for African workers. The processing of cashew involves the following steps: Preliminary cleaning, Roasting, Shelling and separation, Drying, and Peeling.
